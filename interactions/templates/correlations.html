Signaling Pathway Correlations

How Correlations were Calculated

Spearman rank correlations at the single cell level were calcualted across all cell type-pathway pairs (i.e. T-cells Receiving MHC-I). Correlations were filtered such that pathways in a pair shared no functional genes. They were then filtered such that their adjusted p-value < 0.05. These correlations were then collapsed to pathway level correlations by averaging their cell type-pathway pair correlations and taking the best adjusted p-value.

The highest positive correlation in HSCs is between HSCs sending SELPLG and HSCs receiving CD34 (r = 0.736). The highest negative correlations in HSCs is between HSCs sending MHC-I and HSCs sending PARs (r = -0.403).

Plot Usage

Visible pathways can be filtered by adjusting correlation threshold for either positive or negative correlations. Negative correlations are filtered by absolute value correlation. By selecting a correlation, the pathways with correlations greater than the selected threshold, therefore, choosing 0 for both thresholds will show all pathway correlations with adjusted p-value < 0.05.
Select an edge to automatically view the pathway gene networks of the attached nodes on the plots beneath the correlation plot.
